INTRODUCTION

Hi I am a left-wing attorney working at a non-profit that provides free legal services to low income people on housing issues. Prior to going to law school, I studied mathematics and philosophy in undergrad. Ultimately I decided to leave the math field because I did not see the social or political relevance of studying math at the time. My experience and observation taught me that the perceived apoliticalness of the STEM field is both untrue and problematic. Moreover, with the growing right-wing and totalitarian trends in the United States (where I live), I have been increasingly concerned with the survelliance apparatus.

Additionally, I have become acutely aware of my lack of real computer literacy. While I studied math, I was not interested in any other technical field at the time. As a result, my technological and scientific knowledge is woefully inadequate. With the growing proliferation of computers and internet technology, it feels that being proficient in computers is becoming the new literacy.

All of these thoughts have motivated me to study computers. I have created this website to document my journey and to share my interests, projects and what I have learned. I hope you find it interesting!

INTERESTS

What I am interested in learning is a constantly evolving list. As I learn more about computers, I discover more that I do not know. As such, this list will never be complete (probably). Nevertheless, here is my current list of interests with a short explanation of what it is and why I am interested.

  • Web Development

    This one should be fairly self explanatory. Web development is the art/science of making websites. I am interested in it because the internet is ubiquitous and being able to create high quality websites is an incredibly useful skill. Making websites that are visually appealing and easy to use can help contribute to leftist organizing.

  • Mobile App Development

    Mobile app development is the art/science of making applications for the phone. I am interested in learning this as I think there is potential to create mobile apps that can help facilitate left-wing organizing. For example, Signal is a messaging app that has seen increasing activist popularity because of its security and privacy features.

  • Video game development

    Video game development is the programing and design of video games. I like to play video games so being able to make one, or at least understand how they are made would be cool. Plus I might be able to contribute to modding games which can be fun.

  • Networking

    Networking is the field that specializes in setting up connections between computers and connecting these networks to the internet (which is just an even larger connection between computers). My interest stems from the fact that I wish to learn how computers communicate with eachother. This information is useful for anybody interested in cybersecurity as a lot of the vunerabilities for privacy comes from information sharing between computers

  • Hardware

    Hardware is the physical/mechanical aspect of a computer. I am interested in learning how the hardware works as I believe that would provide a deeper understanding of how the software works as well. It will also open more possibilities for projects. Finally, having an understanding of hardware may allow me to provide tech support, which might be useful in leftist spaces

  • Operating Systems

    The operating system is the set of software that supports a computer's basic functions. You cannot truly understand how your computer works without understand how its operating system works. Moreover, I am interested in eventually migrating to a Linux distribution, so understanding how operating systems work can help me in deciding which distribution suits my needs best. I appreciate the idea of free open-source software and would like to participate in the community that uses it and contributes to it.

  • Git and Github

    Git is one of the most popular and powerful Version Control Systems (VCS). VCS is a software that allows developers to manage all the changes made to a project.
    Github is a platform that allows collaboration on a project using Git. Github hosts a plethora of open source code and software which allows for a community of strangers to contribute to eachother's projects. This might be an oversimplification, but you can think of it as a Wikipedia for coding in some ways.

  • Cybersecurity

    Cybersecurity is the field that specializes in protecting internet-connected systems. I am interested in this because data collection by corporations and the government is a serious violation of our privacy rights and leftist activists are especially vunerable to surveillance.

  • Soldering and Electronics

    Soldering is the process of joining two metals together. It is useful for wiring electronics where there is limited space (like in a mobile computer). I am interested in learning this as I would like to work on homemade projects that may require soldering.
